Laws For Muslim

Marriage Law

1. The word Islam means peace and submission. In its religious sense, it denote submission to the Will of God and in its secular sense, it means establishment of peace. Muslims believe in Divine origin of their holly book, which according to they are believed was revealed to the Prophet by Gabriel. The Koran is a Al-furquan meaning one showing truth from falsehood. The Koran was compiled from memory after the Prophet death from the version of third Caliph. Marriage under the Mohammedan law is a civil contract. The essential validity of a marriage is that there should be a proposal made by one party and acceptance by the other. This should be done in the presence of two male or one male and two female witnesses. The proposal and acceptance should be expressed at one meeting.

2. A Muslim marriage is dissolvable. It can be terminated on death or by divorce. Under Muslim husband can have as many as four wives. The only requirement for valid Muslim marriage is that of proposal and acceptance in the presence of witnesses.

3. Remedies available to a husband against a disobedient wife under Muslim Law are divorce, refusal of maintenance and Civil Suit for restitution of conjugal rights.

4. Remedies available to a wife against her husband are suit for maintenance, refusal to live with him, if there is eminent danger to her person and claim to maintenance under Sec. 125 of Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973.
